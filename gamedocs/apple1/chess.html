Microchess

Peter Jennings, 1976 — Apple I port by Larry Nelson — the first commercial microcomputer game

Play chess against the Apple I. The board is displayed as text with a two-digit coordinate system. You play Black (bottom of the board); the computer plays White (top). Originally written for the KIM-1 in 1976, this is believed to be the first commercially sold microcomputer game.

Getting started

  1. The game loads via turbo tape.
  2. Press 0 for Normal difficulty (or 1 Blitz, 2 Super Blitz).
  3. Choose an opening (0–9) or press Return for random play.
  4. Press G to begin. The board is displayed. You move first as Black.

Making a move

Type your move as four digits followed by M, then press Return:

  yxyxM

First two digits = from square, second two = to square. Each square is identified by row (0–7, top to bottom) then column (0–7, left to right).

Example: 6747M moves the piece at row 6, column 7 to row 4, column 7 (Black's rightmost pawn forward two squares).

Tips

  • Start on Normal (0). Blitz and Super Blitz reduce the computer's thinking time and weaken its play significantly.
  • The computer plays at a beginner level. Control the centre, develop your pieces, and castle early.
  • Press P to redraw. If you lose track of positions, redraw the board at any time.
  • Illegal moves are rejected. If you enter an invalid move, the game will reject it — re-enter the correct coordinates.

Apple I keyboard

All input is uppercase. Type commands and press Return.
