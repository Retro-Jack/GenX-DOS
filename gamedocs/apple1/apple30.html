Apple · Apple I

Apple 30th Anniversary

Dave Schmenk, 2006 — 6502 graphics demo posted on Applefritter

A tribute demo written for Apple's 30th anniversary, proving that the Apple I — a machine with no graphics hardware — can still draw pictures. Uses clever character combinations on the 40-column text display to render a recognisable Apple logo and birthday message. As the author put it: "Who said that there was no graphics capability on the Apple 1?"

Getting started

  1. The game loads via turbo tape and runs automatically.
  2. The display builds character by character — sit back and watch.
  3. This is a non-interactive demo. No input needed.

What it shows

  • An ASCII art Apple logo composed from text characters
  • "HAPPY 30TH BIRTHDAY APPLE!" message
  • Decorative borders and patterns

Best appreciated from a bit of distance — the character art resolves into recognisable shapes when you don't stare at individual characters.

Background

  • The Apple I was Steve Wozniak's first computer, hand-built in 1976.
  • It had no graphics mode — just a 40-column text display driven by a terminal chip.
  • Only about 200 units were built. Fewer than 80 are known to survive.
  • This demo was posted on Applefritter.com to celebrate 30 years of Apple Computer (1976–2006).
