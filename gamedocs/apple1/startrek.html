Star Trek

Robert J. Bishop, 1977 — published in Interface Age vol. 2 no. 6

Command the starship Enterprise on a mission to destroy seven Klingon warships hiding in the galaxy. You have 15 stardates, 500 energy units, and 3 photon torpedoes. Dock at starbases to resupply. An 8K Integer BASIC adaptation of the classic 1971 mainframe Star Trek.

Getting started

  1. The game loads via turbo tape and starts automatically.
  2. The galaxy is an 8×8 grid of quadrants. Each quadrant contains an 8×8 grid of sectors.
  3. Enter a command number (0–5) at the prompt and press Return.

Commands

CommandAction
0Move — prompts VECTOR ?. Enter horizontal and vertical sectors to move, e.g. -21,35 moves 21 sectors left and 35 up. Uses energy proportional to distance.
1Short range scan — displays your current quadrant. <*> = Enterprise, +++ = Klingon, >!< = starbase, * = star.
2Long range scan — shows a 3×3 grid of neighbouring quadrants. Each cell reads KBS: K = Klingons, B = starbases, S = stars.
3Fire phasers — shows your remaining energy, then asks how much to fire. Damage decreases with distance to the target.
4Fire photon torpedo — auto-aimed by the computer. Destroys a Klingon in one hit but you only carry 3. The computer avoids hitting stars and starbases.
5Library computer — displays cumulative galactic records or a status report of remaining supplies.

Resources

ResourceStartingNotes

Stardates15Each move costs one stardate. Run out and the mission fails.

Strategy

  • Scan before you move. Use long range scan (command 2) to locate Klingons and plan an efficient route.
  • Save torpedoes for lone Klingons. They're a guaranteed kill but you only have 3. Use phasers when multiple enemies are nearby — phaser fire hits all Klingons in the quadrant.
  • Phaser power vs. distance. Phaser damage drops off with range. Get close before firing, or commit more energy to compensate.
  • Don't over-commit energy to phasers. You need reserves for shields and movement. A phaser shot that uses all your energy leaves you defenceless.
  • Know where your starbases are. Dock to restore energy and torpedoes. Losing both starbases is usually fatal.
  • Watch the clock. 15 stardates for 7 Klingons — you can't afford to wander. Use the library computer (command 5) to review your galactic records.

Apple I keyboard

The Apple I has a standard typewriter keyboard. All input is uppercase. Type command numbers or coordinates and press Return.
