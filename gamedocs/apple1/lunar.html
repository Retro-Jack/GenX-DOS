Lunar Lander

Mark Garetz, 1976 — 6502 assembly lunar landing simulation

Land a spacecraft on the Moon by controlling your thruster burn rate each turn. Too fast and you crash; too little fuel and you plummet. Each turn you see your altitude, velocity, and fuel, then enter a burn rate. Touch down softly to win.

Getting started

  1. The game loads via turbo tape and displays your starting altitude, velocity, and fuel.
  2. Each turn, enter a burn rate (0–30) and press Return.
  3. The game updates your status after each burn.

Display

Each turn shows:

ReadingMeaning
TimeElapsed seconds since descent began
HeightAltitude above the lunar surface (feet)
VelocitySpeed of descent (feet per second) — negative = falling
FuelRemaining fuel units

Burn rates

RateEffect
0No thrust — free-fall under lunar gravity
5Cancels gravity exactly — maintains current velocity
6–29Slows descent (or rises if velocity is already low)
30Maximum burn — decelerates hard but uses fuel fast

Landing outcomes

ResultWhat happened
Perfect landingTouchdown at very low velocity
SurvivedAlive but the craft took damage
CrashToo fast on impact — "you just creamed a 29 megabuck lander"
Out of fuelFuel exhausted mid-descent — at gravity's mercy

Strategy

  • Coast first, brake late. Save fuel for the final approach. Burn at 0 while altitude is high, then increase thrust near the surface.
  • Watch velocity, not altitude. High velocity at low altitude means a crash — start braking early enough.
  • Burn rate 5 holds you steady. Useful for pausing mid-descent to assess your situation.
  • Don't burn at 30 the whole way down. You'll run out of fuel long before landing.

Apple I keyboard

All input is uppercase. Type a number (0–30) and press Return.
