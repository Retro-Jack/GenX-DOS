Castle Wolfenstein

Muse Software, 1981 — Silas Warner — the original stealth-action game

Escape from Castle Wolfenstein, a procedurally-generated Nazi fortress of ~60 rooms. Search chests for supplies, avoid or fight guards, find the war plans, and get out alive. Features digitised speech ("Achtung!", "Schweinhund!") and twin-stick keyboard controls — possibly the first "dual-stick" game, predating Robotron.

Getting started

  1. The game boots from disk. Press Space through the title screen.
  2. Select K for keyboard at the input prompt.
  3. You start in a room with 10 bullets, 3 grenades, and the goal: escape the castle.

Movement (left hand)

Eight-directional movement in a 3×3 grid:

Q ↖W ↑E ↗
A ←S stopD →
Z ↙X ↓C ↘

Aiming (right hand)

Eight-directional aim in a matching grid:

I ↖O ↑P ↗
K ←; →
, ↙. ↓/ ↘

Actions

KeyAction
LFire pistol (in current aim direction)
GThrow grenade (in current aim direction)
SpaceSearch — open chests, search bodies, open doors

Items

ItemUse
BulletsPistol ammunition (start with 10). Loot guards for more.

Enemies

TypeHow to deal with them
Regular guardKilled by pistol. Aim at them and hold still — they may surrender. Search surrendered guards with Space for supplies.
SS StormtrooperWears a bulletproof vest — pistol won't kill them. Must use a grenade. Cannot be fooled by a uniform.

Strategy

  • Save grenades for SS Stormtroopers. Regular guards fall to bullets; SS don't.
  • Hold guards at gunpoint. Aim at them and stand still — they raise their hands. Search them for ammo and keys without spending bullets.
  • Open chests carefully. Shooting a chest open is fast but alerts nearby guards. Keys are silent.
  • Chests contain surprises. Some hold useful items; others just have sauerkraut and schnapps.
  • Find a uniform. Lets you walk past regular guards without fighting — saves ammo for when it matters.
