Karateka

Broderbund, 1984 — Jordan Mechner — cinematic martial arts

Storm the fortress of the warlord Akuma to rescue the Princess Mariko. Fight guards one-on-one using karate punches and kicks, with fluid rotoscoped animation that was groundbreaking for its time. No continues — if you die, you restart from the beginning. From the creator who went on to make Prince of Persia.

Getting started

  1. The game boots from disk with a cinematic introduction.
  2. Press Esc to begin. You start outside Akuma's fortress.
  3. Press → to run forward. Guards attack one at a time.

Combat

  • Both fighters have a life bar at the bottom of the screen. Each hit drains one notch.
  • Health regenerates slowly if neither fighter lands a hit for a few seconds.
  • Kicks reach further than punches but are slower. Use kicks at range, punches up close.
  • Retreating (←) while the enemy attacks reduces damage taken.
  • Mix up high, middle, and low attacks — enemies telegraph their moves.

Hazards

Strategy

  • Run between fights. Use standing mode to cover ground, press Space when a guard approaches.
  • Don't approach Mariko in fighting stance. She kicks you and you die. Press Space to exit fighting stance before reaching her.
  • Let health regenerate. Back off after taking hits — your life bar refills if you avoid combat briefly.
  • Watch for the hawk. It appears suddenly — punch or kick it as it swoops in.
