The Oregon Trail

MECC, 1985 (v1.1) — Don Rawitsch, Bill Heinemann, Paul Dillenberger

Lead a party of five settlers from Independence, Missouri to Oregon's Willamette Valley in 1848. Buy supplies, set your pace, hunt for food, ford rivers, and try to survive 2,000 miles of disease, starvation, broken wagons, and harsh weather. The definitive educational game.

Getting started

  1. The game boots from disk. Choose your occupation — this sets your starting budget and final score multiplier.
  2. Name your party of five (you + four companions).
  3. Buy supplies at Matt's General Store.
  4. Choose your departure month. Too early = cold; too late = snow in the mountains.

On the trail

At each of the 16 landmarks you can:

  • Continue — advance to the next landmark.
  • Change pace — steady, strenuous, or gruelling. Faster wears out your party.
  • Change rations — filling, meagre, or bare bones. Less food = more sickness.
  • Rest — stay put to recover health.
  • Trade — swap items with other travellers.
  • Hunt — shoot game for food (max 100 lbs per hunt).

Hunting

A mini-game where you control a figure and aim in eight directions. Shoot animals for meat — larger animals yield more but are harder to hit. You can carry at most 100 pounds back to the wagon.

Strategy

  • Leave in March or April. Early enough for mountain passes, late enough to avoid spring floods.
  • Buy plenty of oxen. Speed matters — slow travel means winter in the mountains.
  • Stock spare parts. At least 2 of each. Breakdowns without spares can end your game.
  • Hunt early. Supplement food while game is plentiful. Save purchased food for later.
  • Rest when health drops. A few days of rest prevents deaths from accumulated illness.
  • Caulk and float at manageable rivers — it's free. Pay for the ferry at deep crossings.

Apple ][ keyboard

Menu-driven — type numbers or words at prompts and press Return. The hunting mini-game uses single keypresses for aiming.
