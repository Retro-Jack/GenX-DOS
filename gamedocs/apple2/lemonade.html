Lemonade Stand

Bob Jamison / MECC, 1973 — Apple II port by Charlie Kellner, 1979

Run a lemonade stand for 12 days. Each day you decide how many glasses to make, how many advertising signs to buy, and what to charge per glass. Weather, random events, and customer demand change daily. Make as much money as you can. Supports up to 30 independent players taking turns.

Getting started

  1. The game boots from disk. Type Y for instructions or N to skip.
  2. Enter the number of players (1–30) and press Return.
  3. The game runs for 12 days, with a financial summary after each day.

Daily decisions

Each day you see a weather forecast (with a colour illustration), then answer three questions:

PromptWhat to enter
How many glasses to make?Number of glasses to prepare. Each costs supplies from your budget.
How many signs to make?Advertising signs attract customers. More signs = more buyers, but diminishing returns.
Price per glass?Price in cents. Higher prices mean more profit per sale but fewer buyers.

Weather

ForecastEffect on sales
Hot and dryHigh demand — everyone wants cold drinks
SunnyGood demand
CloudyLower demand — risk of thunderstorm

Random events

EventEffect
ThunderstormCan strike on cloudy days. Voids all profits — you lose your entire investment for the day.
Street closureReduces foot traffic past your stand.
WindBlows away some of your advertising signs.

Strategy

  • Match supply to weather. Make more glasses on hot days, fewer on cloudy days.
  • Be cautious on cloudy days. A thunderstorm wipes out everything — keep your investment low.
  • Price reasonably. Too high and nobody buys; too low and you waste profit potential.
  • Advertise on good days. Signs are more effective when weather draws people outside.
  • Don't overproduce. Unsold glasses are wasted. Better to sell out than throw half away.

Apple ][ keyboard

Type numbers at each prompt and press Return.
