Archon

Electronic Arts, 1984 — Jon Freeman, Anne Westfall, Paul Reiche III — chess meets combat

A strategy/action hybrid that plays like chess on a 9×9 board — but when pieces clash, you fight for the square in a real-time combat arena. Light and Dark sides command 18 fantasy units each, from knights and goblins to wizards and dragons. One or two players.

Getting started

  1. The game boots from disk. Choose one or two players and which side to play (Light or Dark).
  2. Move pieces on the board like chess. When you move onto an occupied square, combat begins.
  3. Win by occupying all five power points, eliminating all enemy pieces, or trapping the last one.

Controls (joystick)

In apple2js, move your mouse over the canvas to control direction. If a USB gamepad is connected, it takes over automatically.

The board

  • 9×9 grid with light, dark, and oscillating squares.
  • Luminosity cycle — one-third of the squares shift between light and dark over time. Pieces fight better on their own colour.
  • Five power points — centre of each edge plus the board centre. Occupying them heals your piece and grants spell immunity.

Combat

  • When pieces meet, both enter a full-screen arena for real-time combat.
  • Move your fighter and press the button to attack in the direction you're moving.
  • Square colour matters — the fighter matching the square's current colour gets a health advantage.
  • Stronger pieces usually win, but a weaker piece can prevail with good play.
  • The survivor (possibly wounded) keeps the square.

Strategy

  • Control the power points. They heal your pieces and block enemy spells — holding all five wins the game.
  • Fight on your colour. Time your attacks so combat happens on squares matching your side.
  • Save spells. Each can only be cast once. Revive and Heal are most valuable late-game.
  • Sacrifice weak pieces wisely. A knight/goblin that wounds a strong enemy before dying is a good trade.
